01 — About

The Story So Far

I'm a 4th-year medical student at Saga University School of Medicine in rural Kyushu, Japan. I believe the future of medicine doesn't only live inside hospitals — it lives in the spaces between people, in community ties, in prevention, and in how we show up for one another.

In 2022, I founded SCS (Saga Community Student Association), a student-led group now with 27 members working to strengthen community health in aging rural areas. We run health lectures, brain training, and exercise sessions at public housing complexes and community centers — not as charity, but as co-creation with local residents.

My research focuses on social capital and lonely death prevention, exploring what keeps isolated individuals connected in shrinking communities. I work alongside Dr. Furukawa (Emergency Medicine) and Dr. Yamamoto (ENT), and our findings have been presented at academic conferences with award recognition.

I'm also part of the Irodori+ Button Project, developing a care-monitoring device that bridges clinical observation with technology for real-world social implementation.

02 — Community Work

SCS — Saga Community Student Association

Founded in 2022, SCS is a student-led organization of 27 members dedicated to addressing social isolation in aging rural communities through direct community engagement.

Health Lectures

Delivering accessible health education sessions to elderly residents at public housing complexes, covering topics from hypertension management to fall prevention.

Brain Training Sessions

Interactive cognitive exercises designed to maintain mental acuity and provide social interaction for isolated elderly residents.

Exercise Programs

Group physical activity sessions adapted for various mobility levels at community centers across Saga Prefecture.

Community Outreach

Door-to-door engagement with residents of aging housing complexes, building trust and identifying those at risk of social isolation.

Our Philosophy

We don't see our community work as charity. It's co-creation — building health interventions with local residents, not for them. Every session is designed through dialogue with the community it serves.

03 — Research

Academic Work

Active Research 2022 – Present

Social Capital & Lonely Death Prevention

Saga University School of Medicine

Field research on social isolation in aging housing complexes across Saga Prefecture. This work examines the relationship between social capital — the networks of trust and reciprocity within communities — and the prevention of "kodokushi" (lonely death), a growing crisis in Japan's aging society.

Collaborators
Dr. Furukawa (Emergency Medicine)
Dr. Yamamoto (ENT)
Methods
Field interviews, survey instruments, community mapping
Recognition
Academic conference presentations, award recognition
Active Project 2024 – Present

Irodori+ Button Project

Saga University × Eiwa System Management

A cross-disciplinary collaboration developing a community care-monitoring device. The Irodori+ Button is designed for elderly residents living alone — a simple physical interface that signals well-being to a network of caregivers and neighbors.

My Role
Bridging clinical observation with technology design. Contributing medical and community health perspectives to product development decisions.
Impact Goal
Real-world social implementation of care technology that reduces isolation and supports independent living for elderly residents.
